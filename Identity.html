Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche (born in Saxony, Prussia, October 15, 1844 - died in Weimar, August 25, 1900 at the age of 55) was a German philosopher and a philologist who examined ancient texts, philosophers, cultural critics, poets and composers . He wrote several critical texts on religion, morality, contemporary culture, philosophy and science, displaying a fondness for metaphor, irony and proverb. He was one of the first figures of atheistic modern existentialism.
